The spectral geometry of grokking

One geometric object — the leading spectral edge / eigengap of the rolling parameter-update Gram — governs grokking. It gives early warning of the memorization→generalization transition, is causally implicated in it, encodes the structure of the learned algorithm, and its signature pins down the lock-in stage of an established theoretical framework.

Validated on modular arithmetic, SCAN, and Dyck-1; connected to Yuandong Tian’s Li2 grokking framework, where the σ2/σ3 eigengap fires at epoch 174 ± 1 in 15/15 grok seeds and 0/15 controls.

Last updated June 2026

A theory-anchored landmark

The strongest single result in this program is the one that connects it to a theory that isn’t mine. Yuandong Tian’s Li2 framework (arXiv:2509.21519) describes grokking as a staged dynamics with a “Stage III” lock-in — the point at which feature repulsion becomes dominant. The framework predicts that such a transition exists; it does not, on its own, hand you a clean empirical marker for when it fires.

The σ2/σ3 eigengap of the rolling parameter-update Gram is that marker.

The eigengap pinpoints Tian’s Stage III lock-in: it fires at epoch 174 ± 1 in 15 of 15 grok seeds and 0 of 15 controls, and Theorem 6’s sign-match saturates at the same epoch.

15/15 grok · 0/15 control · ±1 epoch · arXiv:2605.08119

This is the result a skeptical reader should look at first: a binary, falsifiable separation with no overlap between the grok and control distributions, locked to a specific epoch, providing mechanistic verification of a named theorem inside an independent framework. It is the cleanest evidence that the spectral signal is tracking something real about the transition rather than an optimization-geometry artifact.

The signal is causal, not just correlated

Underneath the eigengap is a broader geometric quantity: the commutator defect — the non-commutativity of the operators that govern the training dynamics. It rises before grokking, which makes it a candidate early-warning signal. The load-bearing result is that it is not merely predictive but causal: intervening on it changes the outcome.

Amplifying the commutator defect accelerates grokking by 32–50%; suppressing it prevents grokking entirely. That is an intervention, not a correlation — the geometry is causally implicated in the transition, not a passive readout of it. And it is not an artifact of modular arithmetic: the same early-warning behavior holds on SCAN and Dyck-1, making the signal architecture- and task-agnostic.

What the spectral edge encodes

The spectral edge is not an abstract direction in parameter space — it carries the learned algorithm. Each spectral-edge direction induces a structured function over the input domain: a single Fourier mode for modular addition, a discrete-log mode for multiplication, and a multi-mode family for subtraction. This is structure that standard interpretability tools (including sparse-autoencoder attribution) do not preferentially surface.

Its role changes across the transition. At grokking, the spectral edge converts from a gradient-driven functional axis — the direction along which the function is being learned — into a weight-decay compression axis. After the transition the edge is flat under perturbation, yet >4000× more ablation-critical than random directions: the model has become geometrically robust along the edge while remaining functionally dependent on it. The same direction that carried the learned function becomes the axis along which weight decay compresses the solution.

A bridge to feature learning

To connect this optimizer-geometry picture to the feature-learning literature, per-task gradient SVD links Linear-Centroid features to spectral-edge dynamics with 100–330× coupling — one to two orders of magnitude stronger than the AdamW-update SVD used in prior work. The spectral edge is reading the same structure that feature-learning accounts describe; per-task gradient sensitivity, rather than the optimizer-update spectrum, is the right place to look for it.

Multi-task structure

When several modular-arithmetic tasks grok inside one model, weight decay organizes the dynamics into a phase diagram. Tasks grok in a staggered order, and the joint trajectory concentrates in a 2–6 dimensional global subspace while individual weight matrices remain full-rank — learned algorithms that are globally low-rank in the learning directions but locally full-rank in parameter space. The behavior is consistent with compression onto a shared superposition subspace, with the weight-decay setting controlling where on the phase diagram a run lands.

How this connects to the interpretability program

These results are the parameter-space half of a single claim. The same kind of spectral signal — a gap in the spectrum of a Gram matrix built from training dynamics — that governs phase transitions in parameter space also, applied per-head in activation space, pre-identifies the attention circuits that implement learned behaviors. The grokking work establishes that the signal is causal and theory-anchored; the interpretability program establishes that what an analogous signal identifies are real, ablation-confirmed circuits across an ~20× scale range and two architectures. Two windows — parameter space and activation space — on what appears to be one underlying object.
