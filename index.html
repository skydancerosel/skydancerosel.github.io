AI / ML Research

My current research studies the geometry of neural network training. The core finding is that optimization trajectories of overparameterized transformers collapse onto low-dimensional execution manifolds in weight space. These manifolds are empirically invariant under gradient dynamics: loss-landscape curvature, measured via commutator defects of successive gradient steps, is concentrated entirely in directions orthogonal to the learned subspace. The onset of this transverse curvature reliably predicts generalization transitions (grokking), and the framework extends from single-task to multi-task settings, across training regimes, and to non-arithmetic domains.
